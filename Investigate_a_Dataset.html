Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This dataset contains data on several thousand movies. That includes the plot of the movie, cast, crew, budget, and revenues made from the movie. The aim of this dataset is to predict the success of a movie before its release. We have two related tables:

tmdb_5000_movies¶

This table has the following 20 columns:

  • budget (int64): The budget of the movie.
  • genres (object): The genres of the movie.
  • homepage (object): The official homepage of the movie.
  • id (int64): The unique identifier for the movie.
  • keywords (object): Keywords associated with the movie.
  • original_language (object): The original language of the movie.
  • original_title (object): The original title of the movie.
  • overview (object): A brief summary of the movie.
  • popularity (float64): The popularity score of the movie.
  • production_companies (object): The production companies involved in making the movie.
  • production_countries (object): The countries where the movie was produced.
  • release_date (object): The release date of the movie.
  • revenue (int64): The revenue generated by the movie.
  • runtime (float64): The runtime of the movie in minutes.
  • spoken_languages (object): The languages spoken in the movie.
  • status (object): The release status of the movie.
  • tagline (object): The tagline of the movie.
  • title (object): The title of the movie.
  • vote_average (float64): The average rating of the movie.
  • vote_count (int64): The number of votes the movie has received.

tmdb_5000_credits¶

This table has only 4 columns:

  • movie_id (int64): The unique identifier for the movie.
  • title (object): The title of the movie.
  • cast (object): The cast members of the movie.
  • crew (object): The crew members of the movie.

Both tables share a common column which is id in tmdb_5000_movies and movie_id in tmdb_5000_credits. The tables can be merged on this column. The most important columns would be revenue, budget, and vote_average.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

  • Which factors contribute to higher rating/revenues?
  • Which release months get the highest ratings/revenues?

Conclusions¶

After exploring the data trying to search for factors that contribute to higher average rating or revenue, I came to the following conclusions:

  1. Most movie ratings lie between 5 and 7, with a significant number of 0 ratings, suggesting that a perfect 10/10 rating is very rare, while 0 ratings are more common.
  2. There is a weak positive realtion between the number of votes and the average rating. More votes might slightly lead to higher average rating but the relation has a correlation of 0.3 which isn't very strong.
  3. There is a strong relation between the budget and the revenue of a movie. The statistics indicate that higher budgets lead to higher movie revenues
  4. There is also a strong relation between the number of votes and the revenue of a movie. The more votes a movie gets, the higher its revenue will be
  5. There is no strong relation between revenue and the average vote. This conflicts with the assumption that "good" movies bring more money. In our finding, a higher rating does not necessary lead to a higher revenue
  6. Movies released in January and September tend to have the lowest revenue, while those released in May, June, and November generate the highest revenue.

Limitations¶

  1. Missing Data: The dataset had missing values in several columns. Some rows with missing values were dropped, which might have affected the analysis.
  2. Correlation vs. Causation: The analysis found correlations between various factors, but it is important to note that correlation does not imply causation. Further statistical tests and more detailed data would be needed to establish causative relationships.
